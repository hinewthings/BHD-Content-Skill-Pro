# Content Strategy & Angle

Use this reference to choose the content goal, angle, and concept before writing.

## Content Goals

```txt
Reach: attract new relevant people
Educate: make the market understand the problem or solution
Trust: prove credibility and reduce perceived risk
Consideration: help the customer compare and evaluate
Conversion: drive lead, inbox, booking, trial, or purchase
Retention: onboard, activate, retain, upsell, or request referral
```

## Angle Groups

```txt
Pain angle: "This hurts more than you think."
Desire angle: "This is the outcome you actually want."
Fear angle: "This is the risk of doing nothing."
Mistake angle: "This common mistake blocks results."
Contrarian angle: "The popular belief is incomplete or wrong."
Mechanism angle: "This works because of a specific mechanism."
Proof angle: "Here is evidence, process, or result."
Founder POV angle: "Here is what I learned building/serving."
Comparison angle: "Here is how to choose between options."
Offer angle: "Here is the specific deal and why it is low-risk."
Community angle: "People like you are moving this way."
Future-self angle: "This is how work/life looks after the change."
```

## Content Concepts

Use concepts flexibly, not as hardcoded formats:

```txt
Toplist: curated list of useful points
Best Quotes: pull sharp ideas from a source
Translator: explain complex content in simpler language
Documentary: tell the deeper story or behind-the-scenes
Content Factory: turn one source into many assets
Resource Curation: collect useful resources legally and selectively
POV: personal point of view
Debate: compare two positions
I Am In: identify with a group or movement
Side: take a clear side in a debate
Memories: use past experience or nostalgia
On This Day: connect to a date or event
Born This Day: profile relevant people/events
Fact Check: verify or correct a claim
Mr Comment: turn comments/questions into content
```

## Selection Logic

```txt
If the audience is unaware:
Use pain, symptom, story, mistake, myth-bust, educational angles.

If the audience is problem aware:
Use diagnosis, cost of inaction, mistake, and mechanism angles.

If the audience is solution aware:
Use mechanism, comparison, criteria, proof, and contrarian angles.

If the audience is product aware:
Use proof, demo, founder POV, objection, process, and offer angles.

If the audience is most aware:
Use offer, urgency, risk reversal, bonus, direct CTA, and FAQ angles.
```
