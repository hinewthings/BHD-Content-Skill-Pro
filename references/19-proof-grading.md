# Proof Grading

Use this reference when content depends on trust, performance claims, customer outcomes, case studies, numbers, testimonials, legal statements, or authority claims.

The goal is to decide how strongly the copy is allowed to speak.

## Proof Grades

```txt
Grade A - Strong proof:
Real data, named source, documented case, screenshot, verified testimonial, customer permission, official report, or direct demo.

Grade B - Good proof:
Internal test, anonymized case, process evidence, screen recording, sample output, repeatable benchmark, or clear implementation detail.

Grade C - Soft proof:
Logic proof, checklist, expert reasoning, founder experience, common pattern, or transparent assumption.

Grade D - No proof:
Claim has no supplied evidence, no source, no demo, no case, and no limitation.
```

## Voice Allowed By Proof Grade

```txt
Grade A:
Can use specific numbers and direct claims if scope is clear.

Grade B:
Can use specific but conditional language. Mention "test nội bộ", "case", "trong điều kiện phù hợp", or "nếu setup đúng".

Grade C:
Use "được thiết kế để", "có thể giúp", "mục tiêu là", "theo logic", "quy trình này giúp".

Grade D:
Do not use numbers, guarantees, superiority, legal certainty, or customer outcomes. Use softer educational or process-led copy.
```

## Proof Upgrade Suggestions

If proof is weak, suggest one of these:

```txt
Demo proof:
Show screen recording, sample output, before-after draft, workflow walkthrough.

Process proof:
Show checklist, SOP, scoring rubric, decision tree, or mechanism.

Data proof:
Show time period, sample size, campaign context, baseline, result, and limitation.

Customer proof:
Use testimonial, feedback screenshot, anonymized case, permission-based quote.

Comparison proof:
Compare old workflow vs new workflow with controlled assumptions.
```
