# Audience & Insight

Use this reference to avoid generic content.

## Core Questions

Analyze:

```txt
1. Who is the customer?
2. What do they want?
3. What pain is already visible?
4. What hidden fear is underneath?
5. What have they tried before?
6. What do they not believe yet?
7. What proof would make them trust?
8. How do they want to see themselves?
9. What stops them from acting?
10. What trigger makes them act now?
```

## Six Insight Layers

```txt
Pain: obvious problem, frustration, loss, inefficiency
Fear: hidden anxiety, risk, shame, regret, being left behind
Desire: wanted outcome, relief, growth, status, control
Identity: how the customer wants to see themselves
Friction: reason they delay, avoid, compare, or distrust
Trigger: event, deadline, pain spike, opportunity, seasonal pressure
```

## Strong Insight Test

A strong insight should make the target customer feel:

```txt
"Đúng, đây là vấn đề của mình."
"Mình từng nghĩ vậy."
"Đây là lý do mình vẫn chưa hành động."
```

If the insight could apply to anyone, sharpen the audience or situation.

## Audience Sophistication

Classify the audience's maturity before choosing language depth, proof type, and CTA.

```txt
Level 1 - Beginner:
Needs simple language, concrete examples, low-friction CTA, and minimal jargon.

Level 2 - Practitioner:
Understands common terms and daily pain. Can handle specific tactics, checklists, and workflow details.

Level 3 - Senior:
Needs mechanism, edge cases, tradeoffs, proof quality, limitations, and sharper claims.

Level 4 - Founder / Decision-maker:
Needs ROI logic, operational impact, risk, cost, speed, ownership, and implementation path.

Level 5 - Enterprise:
Needs governance, compliance, integration depth, procurement risk, data security, and cross-team adoption.
```

## Sophistication Rules

```txt
Beginner:
Translate jargon before using it.

Practitioner:
Use tactical specifics and examples from the work.

Senior:
Avoid hype. Add caveats, proof grades, and mechanism.

Founder / Decision-maker:
Tie features to cost, speed, control, team output, or revenue path.

Enterprise:
Prioritize compliance, process, data, ownership, and risk controls.
```
