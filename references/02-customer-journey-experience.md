# Customer Journey & Experience Layers

Use this reference for every strategy, funnel, ads, landing page, campaign, and channel-growth request. Content should match where the customer is in their decision journey and which experience layer needs improvement.

## Customer Journey Stages

```txt
Unaware:
The customer does not know they have the problem. Use education, symptoms, mistakes, stories, and market awareness.

Problem Aware:
The customer knows the problem but does not know the solution. Use pain clarity, cost of inaction, common mistakes, and simple diagnosis.

Solution Aware:
The customer knows solution categories. Use mechanism, comparison, selection criteria, how-to, and proof of approach.

Product Aware:
The customer knows the brand/product but is not convinced. Use case studies, demos, objections, guarantees, process, and offer clarity.

Most Aware:
The customer is close to buying. Use deadline, bonus, risk reversal, FAQ, direct CTA, and decision support.

Purchased:
The customer has bought. Use onboarding, next steps, activation, success checklist, usage content, and expectation setting.

Retention / Advocacy:
The customer needs continued value. Use result review, advanced tips, community, upsell, referral, testimonial request, and loyalty content.
```

## Experience Layers

```txt
Awareness Experience:
First impression. Does the customer quickly understand who this is for and why it matters?

Education Experience:
Learning. Does the customer understand the problem, the stakes, and the solution logic?

Trust Experience:
Credibility. Does the customer see proof, process, people, and realistic claims?

Consideration Experience:
Evaluation. Does the customer have enough clarity to compare options?

Conversion Experience:
Action. Is the offer clear, the CTA easy, and the risk low?

Onboarding Experience:
Post-action. Does the customer know what happens next?

Result Experience:
Progress. Does the customer see early wins or measurable movement?

Loyalty Experience:
Relationship. Does the customer have reasons to stay, refer, upgrade, or advocate?
```

## Mapping Rules

```txt
Unaware + Awareness:
Use hooks, symptoms, stories, market education.

Problem Aware + Education:
Use diagnosis, cost of inaction, mistakes, simple frameworks.

Solution Aware + Consideration:
Use mechanism, criteria, comparison, myth busting.

Product Aware + Trust:
Use proof, case studies, demo, process, FAQ.

Most Aware + Conversion:
Use offer, bonus, deadline, guarantee, low-friction CTA.

Purchased + Onboarding:
Use welcome sequence, next steps, implementation checklist.

Retention + Loyalty:
Use advanced value, wins, community, referral, upsell.
```

## If User Does Not Know The Journey

Ask:

```txt
Khách hàng của bạn hiện giống nhóm nào nhất?
A. Họ chưa biết vấn đề này quan trọng
B. Họ biết vấn đề nhưng chưa biết nên làm gì
C. Họ đang tìm hoặc so sánh giải pháp
D. Họ biết bạn rồi nhưng chưa đủ tin để mua
E. Họ đã mua và cần được chăm sóc tốt hơn
Hoặc mô tả tình huống thực tế.
```

Ask experience layer:

```txt
Bạn thấy điểm nghẽn lớn nhất nằm ở đâu?
A. Người mới nhìn vào chưa hiểu bạn giúp gì
B. Khách chưa hiểu vì sao họ cần giải pháp
C. Khách chưa đủ tin
D. Khách muốn mua nhưng hành động còn khó
E. Khách mua xong chưa được dẫn dắt tốt
Hoặc mô tả điểm nghẽn riêng.
```
