# Behavioral Persuasion

Use persuasion responsibly. The goal is to clarify value, reduce friction, and motivate action without fabricating claims or exploiting vulnerable people.

## Psychological Levers

```txt
Social Proof: people trust what others use. Use reviews, usage, case studies.
Authority Bias: people trust expertise. Use credentials, process, publications, experts.
Scarcity: limited availability increases perceived value. Use only if true.
Urgency: deadlines motivate action. Use only if true.
Loss Aversion: people avoid loss more strongly than they seek gain.
Anchoring: first reference point shapes value perception.
Framing: the same fact can be understood differently depending on framing.
Reciprocity: giving useful value first increases openness.
Commitment: small actions lead to larger actions.
Consistency: people act in line with their self-image.
Mere Exposure: repeated useful presence increases familiarity.
Halo Effect: good presentation improves perceived credibility.
Contrast Effect: comparison clarifies value.
Peak-End Rule: people remember the strongest moment and ending.
Curiosity Gap: an open loop earns attention.
Cognitive Ease: simple content feels more believable.
Endowment Effect: personalized preview increases perceived ownership.
Zeigarnik Effect: incomplete loops keep attention.
```

## Irrational Buying Motivations

```txt
Fear of waste: "I am losing money/time/data."
Fear of falling behind: "Competitors are moving faster."
Identity upgrade: "This is who I want to become."
Control desire: "I want visibility and control."
Simplicity bias: "I want fewer moving parts."
Status signal: "This makes me look more professional."
Belonging: "People like me use this."
Future self: "My future workflow/life is better."
Certainty: "I feel safer because the process is clear."
Convenience: "This saves effort."
```

## Application Rules

```txt
Use one primary lever and one supporting lever.
Tie each lever to a real audience insight.
Do not add fake scarcity, fake urgency, fake social proof, or fake authority.
Prefer clear value over pressure.
```
