# Marketing Canvas

Use this reference when the user needs campaign strategy, channel strategy, roadmap, positioning, content pillars, message development, or when the brief is too tactical and lacks marketing logic.

The canvas turns a content request into a strategy:

```txt
Goal -> Customer -> Proposition -> Message -> Levers -> Channels -> Metrics -> Roadmap
```

## 1. Goal: SMART Marketing Objective

Clarify the business and marketing goal before writing.

SMART means:

```txt
Specific: what exactly should change?
Measurable: which metric proves progress?
Achievable: is it realistic with current resources?
Relevant: does it support the business priority?
Time-bound: by when?
```

Common marketing goals:

```txt
Brand awareness
New product/service promotion
Product launch
Recognition and recall
Customer loyalty
Revenue contribution
Attraction / acquisition
Conversion
Engagement
```

Guided question:

```txt
Mục tiêu marketing chính là gì?
A. Tăng nhận diện thương hiệu
B. Ra mắt hoặc quảng bá sản phẩm/dịch vụ mới
C. Tạo lead, inbox, booking hoặc chuyển đổi
D. Tăng gắn kết hoặc lòng trung thành
E. Đóng góp doanh thu có đo lường
Hoặc nhập mục tiêu riêng.
```

## 2. Customer: Start From Jobs To Be Done

Do not define customers only by demographic traits. Prioritize the customer's job to be done.

Analyze four job layers:

```txt
Functional job:
What practical task or outcome do they need done?

Emotional job:
What feeling are they trying to gain or avoid?

Personal job:
What kind of person do they want to become or prove to themselves?

Social job:
How do they want others to see them?
```

Also estimate segment volume:

```txt
Large enough to build a campaign?
Specific enough to write sharp content?
Painful enough to motivate action?
Reachable through available channels?
```

Guided question:

```txt
Khách hàng thuê/mua sản phẩm để hoàn thành "việc cần làm" nào?
A. Giải quyết một vấn đề vận hành/chức năng
B. Giảm lo lắng, áp lực, rủi ro hoặc cảm giác mất kiểm soát
C. Trở thành phiên bản chuyên nghiệp/thông minh/tự tin hơn
D. Được công nhận hoặc được nhìn nhận tốt hơn trong mắt người khác
E. Tôi chưa rõ, hãy giúp xác định JTBD từ brief
Hoặc nhập job cụ thể.
```

## 3. Proposition: Why Buy And Why Buy From Us

A strong proposition must answer:

```txt
Why should the customer buy this product/service?
Why should the customer buy from this brand/team instead of alternatives?
```

It should:

```txt
Target a clear customer group.
Persuade prospects that they receive better value than alternatives.
State the value mechanism, not only the feature list.
```

For B2C, emphasize:

```txt
Personal outcome
Emotion
Convenience
Identity
Risk reduction
Immediate perceived value
```

For B2B, emphasize:

```txt
Business result
Cost/risk reduction
Operational efficiency
Measurability
Process reliability
Decision confidence
```

The proposition should deliver at least one of four value effects:

```txt
Pain reduction: reduce the customer's pain.
Pain removal: remove a painful blocker completely.
Short-term pleasure: create an immediate win or relief.
Long-term gain: create durable improvement or future advantage.
```

Guided question:

```txt
Đề xuất giá trị chính nên nằm ở đâu?
A. Giảm đau/rủi ro cho khách hàng
B. Loại bỏ một điểm nghẽn lớn
C. Tạo lợi ích nhanh, dễ thấy
D. Tạo lợi ích dài hạn/bền vững
E. Chưa rõ, hãy đề xuất từ insight và offer
Hoặc nhập đề xuất riêng.
```

## 4. Message: Communication Structure

A good message should not be boring. It must create attention, interest, desire, commitment, and action.

Message components:

```txt
Headline
Interest builder
Problem or opportunity
Before-after or reason
Benefit and outcome
Proof
Commitment or desire trigger
CTA
```

Headline types:

```txt
Question
Warning
How-to
Test/quiz
News
Evidence-led
Contrarian
Specific benefit
```

Style choices:

```txt
Fun
Serious
Emotional
Humorous
Trend-led
Founder/real person
Expert/direct
```

Every message must answer:

```txt
Why should I buy?
Why should I buy from you?
```

Guided question:

```txt
Thông điệp nên mở theo hướng nào?
A. Đặt câu hỏi khiến khách tự soi lại vấn đề
B. Cảnh báo rủi ro hoặc chi phí của việc không hành động
C. Chỉ cách làm/how-to rõ ràng
D. Dùng dẫn chứng, chứng thực hoặc bằng chứng
E. Dùng góc nhìn trend, vui, hài hước hoặc gây tranh luận
Hoặc nhập hướng thông điệp riêng.
```

## 5. Connection Levels

Content can connect at four levels:

```txt
Rational:
Logic, features, price, process, data.

Emotional:
Fear, desire, relief, pride, frustration, hope.

Spiritual / Meaning:
Purpose, values, mission, identity, belonging.

Belief / Deeper worldview:
What the customer believes about themselves, work, success, status, or the future.
```

Practical rule:

```txt
People often buy through emotion and identity, then justify with rational reasons.
```

Use rational proof to reduce risk, but do not rely on rational proof alone when the product is aspirational, personal, or status-driven.

## 6. Levers

Use levers to make content more noticeable. Use them responsibly and match compliance rules.

```txt
Difference: a distinctive factor that makes the offer stand out.
Attraction: visual, emotional, or outcome appeal.
Contrast: before-after, old-new, manual-automated, chaos-control.
Fame: expert, brand, KOL/KOC, public recognition.
Trend: current event, behavior, platform trend.
Sex appeal: high-risk; avoid unless brand/category and platform allow.
Controversy: use for thoughtful debate, not attacks or unsupported claims.
```

Guided question:

```txt
Bạn muốn dùng đòn bẩy nào?
A. Sự khác biệt nổi bật
B. Tương phản trước/sau hoặc cũ/mới
C. Trend hoặc bối cảnh thị trường
D. Dẫn chứng từ chuyên gia/KOL/KOC/khách hàng
E. Tranh luận/phản biện một quan điểm phổ biến
Hoặc nhập đòn bẩy riêng.
```

## 7. Channels

Group channels into three types:

```txt
Paid:
Facebook Ads, TikTok Ads, Google Ads, KOL booking, sponsored content.

Owned:
Website, landing page, email list, fanpage, TikTok channel, group, CRM, blog.

Earned:
Shares, mentions, reviews, referrals, press, community conversations, organic comments.
```

Channel strategy should lead customers:

```txt
From not knowing -> understanding -> trusting -> considering -> buying -> using -> advocating.
```

Guided question:

```txt
Kênh chính của chiến dịch là gì?
A. Paid: Facebook/TikTok/Google Ads
B. Owned: website, landing page, fanpage, email, CRM
C. Earned: review, referral, community, PR, organic share
D. Kết hợp paid + owned + earned
E. Chưa rõ, hãy đề xuất kênh theo mục tiêu và khách hàng
Hoặc nhập kênh riêng.
```

## 8. Metrics

Use three metric groups:

```txt
Customer metrics:
Reach, impressions, engagement, follower growth, profile visits, comments, saves, shares, sentiment.

Pipeline metrics:
Leads, inboxes, form submissions, bookings, trials, qualified leads, conversion rate by stage, response time.

Financial metrics:
Revenue contribution, CAC, ROAS, cost per lead, cost per booking, average order value, LTV, payback period.
```

Match metrics to goal:

```txt
Awareness: reach, recall, impressions, video views.
Engagement: comments, saves, shares, watch time.
Lead generation: CPL, lead quality, booking rate.
Conversion: CVR, CAC, ROAS, revenue.
Retention: repeat purchase, usage, referral, testimonial rate.
```

Guided question:

```txt
Bạn muốn đo thành công bằng nhóm chỉ số nào?
A. Khách hàng: reach, engagement, follower, sentiment
B. Pipeline: lead, inbox, booking, trial, conversion rate
C. Tài chính: doanh thu, CAC, ROAS, CPL, LTV
D. Cả 3 nhóm
E. Chưa rõ, hãy đề xuất KPI theo mục tiêu
Hoặc nhập KPI riêng.
```
